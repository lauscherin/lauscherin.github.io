Research Interests

My research is focused on the comparative and bioinformatic analysis of molecular data of diverse eukaryotic genomes to detect inter- and intra-species differences (e.g. in the amount and distribution of transposed elements, numts, and retropseudogenes). Furthermore, I'm interested in the development of user-friendly softwares especially for the analysis of huge NGS-datasets.

Bioinformatics

Since the end of 2011, I'm more and more interested in Bioinformatics due to the growing influence of Next Generation Sequening data and their analysis.

Molecular Genetics

The main topic during my diploma studies was "Molecular Genetics". My diploma thesis was mostly done in the laboratory. Therefore, I'm not only a "Computer-Nerd" but also able to work in a wet lab.

Anthropology & Primatology

One of my favorite topics enclosing "Bioinformatics" and "Molecular Genetics" is the evolution of humans and all other primates. Even in my current projects, I'm working at such species.
